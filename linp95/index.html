Digital Beats: The Journey of Music in the Streaming Era
By Lechuan Meng, Lin Zhu, Hansheng Huang

Lay's Story

Lay, with her luminescent silver hair, spends her evenings lost in music videos. She doesn't play any instruments, but through the glow of her computer screen, she feels every note and beat. Music is her escape, her silent passion. As the melodies play, her face reflects the spectrum of emotions they stir—her world is painted in the hues of the music she adores, a private concert in her quiet room.

Enthralled by the music videos of two popular singers from her town, Lay watches with a mix of admiration and longing. She envies their confidence and stage presence, dreaming of the day she can share her own music with the world just like her idols. These videos aren't just entertainment for Lay; they're a window to the dream she's quietly nurturing within.

In her dream, Lay is a dazzling singer on stage, but awake, she's clouded with uncertainty about how to start her music career. This dream versus reality shows her desire and doubt, a common struggle for anyone chasing a dream.

Resolved to chase her dreams, Lay turns to Spotify for guidance, scouring through playlists and artist data, trying to decode the secret to a successful start in her music career. Each click is a step towards her goal, her eyes a blend of determination and hope.

Key Features

There are eleven features often found in music datasets can be described as metrics that evaluate a song's danceability, energy, key, loudness, speechiness, acousticness, instrumentalness, liveness, valence, tempo, and duration, providing a comprehensive analysis of its musical characteristics and listener experience.

Danceability

"Danceability" in Spotify's context quantifies how suitable a song is for dancing, taking into account factors like tempo and rhythm, with values ranging from 0.0 (least danceable) to 1.0 (most danceable). This metric is valuable for those interested in music for dancing, such as DJs or playlist creators, looking to compile tracks for lively settings.

Upon examining the danceability data, we find the median score is 0.637, indicating most songs are reasonably danceable. The interquartile range (0.518 to 0.7405) reflects a consistency in danceability, suggesting a dataset with a good mix of danceable tracks. With scores stretching from 0 (least danceable) to 0.975 (highly danceable), the dataset appears to cater well to dance enthusiasts

Energy

"Energy" is a metric on Spotify that reflects the intensity and activity level of a track, scored between 0.0 and 1.0. High-energy tracks are typically fast, loud, and vibrant, whereas a track with lower energy might be slower and more subdued. The energy level can contribute to a song's feel, affecting how listeners might respond to it—high-energy tracks could energize workouts or dance floors, while low-energy music might be preferred for relaxed or reflective settings.

The box plot for "Energy" shows a median value of 0.666, indicating that the central tendency of tracks in this dataset leans towards a higher energy level. Most tracks fall within a range of 0.507 to 0.798 (the interquartile range), demonstrating a general trend towards more energized music. The presence of tracks with the maximum energy value of 1 suggests that there are highly intense tracks suitable for high-energy environments. Conversely, the minimum value is quite low at approximately 0.2, implying the dataset includes a few much less intense tracks, which could serve a different listener demographic or mood.

Key

In music, the "Key" of a track refers to the group of pitches, or scale, that forms the basis of a music composition. In Spotify's dataset, 'Key' is measured using standard Pitch Class notation where each number corresponds to a specific pitch; for example, 0 is C, 1 is C♯/D♭, 2 is D, and so on up to 11, which represents B. This information is useful for musicians, DJs, and musicologists who are interested in the tonal characteristics of music and how it might influence the listening experience.

The box plot for "Key" shows a median value of 5, which corresponds to the pitch class of F. The interquartile range extends from 2 (D) to 8 (G♯/A♭), indicating that half of the tracks in the dataset are distributed among these keys. The full range goes from 0 (C) to 11 (B), showing that the dataset includes a complete variety of musical keys. This variety allows for a broad representation of music and can be indicative of the diversity in the harmonic structure of the songs included in the dataset.

Loudness

"Loudness" reflects the average volume of a song in decibels (dB) and is crucial in music production and user experience. It influences how we perceive the intensity of a song; a higher dB level usually indicates a louder and potentially more energetic track. This metric is valuable for ensuring consistency in playlists and understanding the dynamics of different music genres.

The provided loudness box plot indicates a median of -6.536 dB, suggesting a relatively moderate loudness level for the central track in the dataset. The interquartile range from -8.858 dB to -4.931 dB shows a typical spread for song loudness, while the minimum and maximum values (-46.251 dB to 0.92 dB) display an extensive range, pointing to a diverse set of tracks from very quiet to quite loud within the collection.

Speechiness

"Speechiness" measures the presence of spoken words in a song, with a scale from 0 to 1. Tracks with a high score close to 1 would consist mainly of spoken words, like talk shows or audiobooks, while those with a score closer to 0 are likely to be mostly music. This feature helps differentiate between songs with more lyrical content from those that are instrumental, impacting the selection for playlists aimed at specific listening experiences, like workout or relaxation.

From the box plot, we see that the median value of speechiness is around 0.0505, suggesting that the average song in the dataset has few spoken words. The majority of tracks have a speechiness value below 0.103, indicating a collection that is largely musical in nature. However, there's at least one track with a value near the maximum of 0.964, which would be considered highly speech-centric. This wide range suggests the dataset encompasses a broad spectrum of tracks, from instrumental to those featuring significant spoken elements.

Acousticness

"Acousticness" measures the likelihood of a track being acoustic, with a value between 0.0 and 1.0 indicating the confidence level of the track's acoustic nature. High acousticness suggests a track has few to no electronic sounds, appealing to listeners who enjoy unplugged or organic music. It's a valuable feature for music discovery services aiming to categorize songs by sound and texture.

The box plot for acousticness shows a median of 0.193, which means the majority of tracks have a low probability of being acoustic. The interquartile range from 0.0452 to 0.4775 indicates a significant number of tracks have some degree of acousticness, yet still lean towards electronic production. With the maximum value near 1 (0.996), there are tracks in the dataset with a high likelihood of being fully acoustic, catering to a niche audience that prefers pure acoustic music.

Instrumentalness

"Acousticness" measures the confidence that a song is acoustic, on a scale from 0.0 to 1.0, with higher values indicating a greater likelihood of the track being acoustic. This metric is significant for distinguishing between electronic and acoustic sounds, offering insight into the production style of music.

Looking at the provided box plot, the median acousticness value is 0.193, suggesting that, on average, tracks have some acoustic elements but tend to be more electronic. The data spread, with a first quartile (Q1) at 0.0452 and a third quartile (Q3) at 0.4775, shows a wide range of acousticness, indicating a diverse mix of songs in the dataset. A high maximum value of 0.996 suggests some tracks are almost entirely acoustic, while the presence of values close to 0 highlights the inclusion of highly electronic music.

Liveness

"Liveness" is a measure used by Spotify to estimate the probability that the track was performed live. The scale runs from 0.0 to 1.0, with higher values suggesting a higher chance that the song features live recorded sounds, which may involve audience noise, live performance variations, and the unfiltered acoustics typical of a live setting.

The box plot for liveness indicates a median of 0.125, meaning that the central value of tracks in this dataset is skewed towards lower liveness, suggesting that studio-recorded tracks are more common than live ones. The range from the first quartile at 0.0941 to the third quartile at 0.237 signifies that there's not a large variation in liveness in the majority of tracks. However, the maximum value of 1 suggests that there are some tracks with very high liveness, which could be actual live recordings or tracks with elements that give the feel of a live performance.

Valence

"Valence" is a musical attribute measured by Spotify, indicating the musical positiveness conveyed by a track. Scores range from 0.0 to 1.0, where high valence reflects a more positive, upbeat sound—think cheerful or euphoric—while low valence indicates a more negative, darker mood.

The box plot for valence shows a median of 0.537, suggesting a balance in the dataset between high-valence, positive-sounding tracks and low-valence, less cheerful ones. The interquartile range extends from 0.339 to 0.7265, indicating that the majority of tracks have a moderate to high valence. However, the presence of tracks with valence close to the maximum (0.993) points to some very positive songs, while a minimum at 0 indicates the inclusion of tracks with very low positivity.

Tempo

"Tempo" in music refers to the speed at which a piece is played, measured in beats per minute (BPM). A higher BPM indicates a faster tempo, suitable for high-energy dance music, while a lower BPM suggests a slower pace, common in genres like ballads or ambient music. Tempo is a fundamental aspect of a track's character and influences how listeners experience the rhythm and mood of the music.

The tempo box plot reveals a median of 120 BPM, fitting for pop and dance music, with most tracks between 97 and 140 BPM. A maximum of over 240 BPM points to some extremely fast tracks, while a minimum of 0 suggests possible data errors or very slow, ambient music.

Duration

"Duration" quantifies the length of a song in milliseconds. It's a straightforward but vital metric, providing insights into the song's structure and potential listener engagement. Typically, duration impacts where a song fits in playlists and broadcasts, with different lengths suiting various contexts.

The duration box plot indicates a median song length of about 3.5 minutes, typical for pop music. The range for most songs is from 3 to around 4.2 minutes. An outlier exists with a duration of over an hour, possibly an extended mix, while the shortest track is just over 30 seconds, likely a brief interlude or preview.
